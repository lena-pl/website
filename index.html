Kia ora! RailsBridge is a free weekend workshop for underrepresented peoples looking to get a taste of tech. We provide a warm and encouraging environment as we run you through tutorials and activities about coding in Ruby, Rails, and whatever else might catch your interest.

Applications are open from June 25th. Put it in your calendar!

Who is this for?

This is primarily for women, girls and non-binary peoples who are beginners to coding and want to try it our and see what it’s all about. We welcome Māori and Pacifika, those in LGBTQIA+ communities, and any who are underrepresented in tech. We’re here to support YOU!

RailsBridge is open to people of all ages, but we prefer those 13 or under to come with a guardian.

How must does it cost?

Thanks to our wonderful sponsors, nothing! It's free! Just show up with a laptop and enthusiasm to learn! We can also help with public transport costs if you need, too.

Can men attend?

Yes, but women, non-binary folk, and people of colour will be given priority.

Do you have a Code of Conduct?

Yes - here is the Code of Conduct. We expect all attendees, coaches, sponsor representatives, volunteers and organisers to behave respectfully so everyone has a great time. If you run into any issues, don't hesitate to contact any one of the organisers.

I can program! May I help?

Yes! We're also looking for coaches to to help out with teaching and sharing the passion for coding. Coach applications can be found here.
